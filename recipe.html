CORDON BLEU
Chicken cordon bleu is a popular dish of boneless chicken breast pounded and rolled around a pork meat (such as ham or prosciutto) and a soft cheese (such as mozzarella, Swiss or blue cheese) and then either fried or baked. Chicken cordon bleu is a relatively recent American, drawing on both techniques from Chicken Kiev and other Schnitzel dishes. The earliest reference to Chick Cordon Bleu is dated to 1967, while similar veal recipes are found from at least 1955 the french term is translated from "Blue ribbon" the chicken dish should not be confused with the school of the same name.
